Inside Stars:

Inside a star, extremely high temperatures and pressure cause a process called nuclear fusion. During fusion, hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing huge amounts of energy. This energy travels from the core to the surface and is released as light and heat.

A star has several layers:

  • Core – the hottest part, where nuclear fusion takes place.
  • Radiative Zone – energy moves outward as radiation.
  • Convective Zone – hot gases rise and cooler gases sink, carrying energy to the surface.
  • Photosphere – the visible surface of the star.
  • Corona – the outer atmosphere of the star.

The core of the Sun reaches temperatures of about 15 million°C, making it one of the hottest places in our solar system.
